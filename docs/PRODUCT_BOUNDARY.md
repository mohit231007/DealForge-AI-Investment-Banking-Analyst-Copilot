# Public / Private Product Boundary

## Public portfolio repository

This repository exists to demonstrate Mohit Bhatnagar's ability to design and build a finance workflow product.

It may include:

- synthetic CSV inputs;
- deterministic validation logic;
- adjusted valuation calculations;
- sample Office exports;
- Streamlit demonstration UI;
- automated tests;
- architecture and case-study documentation.

It intentionally excludes:

- confidential data-room processing;
- private company documents;
- commercial connector credentials;
- paid market-data integrations;
- authentication, billing, and entitlements;
- proprietary product scoring and orchestration;
- internal deployment and security configuration;
- customer-specific workflows.

## Private Algosphere product

The private repository `DealForge-AI` is the commercial product base for **DealForge by The Algosphere**.

Planned private capabilities include:

- persistent deal workspaces;
- document ingestion and source lineage;
- user, reviewer, and admin roles;
- saved versions and approval workflow;
- freemium/subscription entitlements;
- organization-level security controls;
- public and licensed data connectors;
- private cloud or customer-hosted deployment;
- operational monitoring and audit logs.

## Why the split matters

The public version must be understandable, reproducible, and safe for recruiters to inspect. The private product must protect intellectual property, customer information, connector logic, security design, and commercial differentiation.

The two repositories should share product principles, not necessarily identical source code.
